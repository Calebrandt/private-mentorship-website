Overview

Childcare under Custom Plans combines afterschool care, tutoring, and the daily structure of family life into one ongoing relationship. Your child has the same trusted person every day. The Assistant handles pickups, homework, meal prep, and the moving parts of a busy household — so parents come home to a steady, calm afternoon rather than a scramble.

This is not babysitting. The Assistant is a long-term presence in your child's life, matched on personality and academic fit, supported by a written contract.

What the Assistant helps with

  • School drop-offs and pickups at agreed times
  • Homework support and tutoring in all standard subjects, K–12 and post-secondary
  • Meal prep and snacks — healthy after-school food, dinner help when asked
  • Driving to appointments, activities, and friends' houses
  • Reading, project, and creative time — structured screen-light afternoons
  • Sibling coordination when there's more than one child
  • Light household tasks while children are at school — laundry, tidying, errands

How childcare works

The Assistant arrives at the time agreed during intake — most families use afterschool through dinner, or pickup through bedtime. The same Assistant comes every day. The schedule is documented and stays consistent week to week. If the schedule needs to shift mid-year (a new activity, a sport season), we update it together with thirty days' notice.

What about school breaks and holidays?

Full-day coverage is available during school breaks. Some families build it into their tier upfront; others add it ad-hoc with a week's notice. Holidays are documented in the contract.

Can the Assistant tutor in specific subjects?

Yes. Tutoring is part of the role, not an add-on. The Assistant is matched in part on what your child is studying — math focus, ESL, post-secondary essay prep, language work. For specialized subjects beyond the Assistant's training, we'll be upfront on the intake call.

Are the Assistants vetted for working with children?

Every Assistant clears a criminal record check including the vulnerable sector check, references, and an in-person vetting interview before joining a family. Same standard on every contract.

Common questions

What ages do you support?

Kindergarten through Grade 12 is the core. We also work with post-secondary students and young adults through age twenty-five — many of our long-term families keep their Assistant through university and the first job.

Can the Assistant teach my child a specific subject?

Yes — tutoring is part of the role. At intake we ask which subjects matter most and match accordingly. For very specialised content (advanced math, niche language) we'll tell you upfront whether we have the right person.

Are weekends and evenings available?

Yes, with intake. Most childcare contracts are weekdays only, but evenings and weekends can be built in or added later with thirty days' notice.

Will the Assistant discipline my child?

No. The Assistant follows your household's expectations and structure — they're not a disciplinarian. Behaviour issues outside daily friction are escalated to you, not handled independently.

What if my child and the Assistant don't bond?

We rematch within the contract at no charge during the first month. Most fit issues surface in the first two weeks, and we'd rather find out then than three months in.

Can the Assistant drive my child?

Yes — drop-offs, pickups, activities, and friend visits. Drivers are insured and licensed. We use the family vehicle when available; otherwise the Assistant's own.
