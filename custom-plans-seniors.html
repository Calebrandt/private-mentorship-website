Overview

The goal of senior care at Private Mentorship is to maintain your parent's safety and comfort at home — letting them keep up their daily routine while receiving careful assistance with the tasks that have grown harder. A long-term Private Family Assistant supports personal hygiene, meal preparation, light homemaking, medication reminders, transportation to appointments, and companionship.

Short-term live-in care is available for transitions, recoveries, and times when family travel takes you out of town. A rotation of two or three Assistants provides 24-hour presence so your parent is never alone.

What the Assistant helps with

  • Personal hygiene and bathing assistance with dignity
  • Meal preparation — healthy meals, snacks, hydration reminders
  • Light homemaking — laundry, dishes, tidying, linens
  • Medication reminders at scheduled times (we do not administer)
  • Transportation to medical appointments, the pharmacy, and personal errands
  • Companionship — walks, meals, reading, conversation, outings
  • Coordination with family members and the existing healthcare team

How short-term live-in care works

Before live-in care begins, prepare a private room where the Assistant can rest during their stay. We schedule a rotation of two or three Assistants — each remains with your parent for several days at a time before handing off to the next. The schedule is agreed during intake and adjusted as needs evolve.

How daily visits work (non-live-in)

Visits are scheduled in 4- to 12-hour blocks on a consistent weekday pattern. The same Assistant comes every day so your parent builds trust with one person. The schedule is set at intake and stays steady week to week.

Hand-offs between Assistants

When live-in Assistants rotate, the outgoing Assistant briefs the incoming one in person. Medication times, meal preferences, mood, and any incidents from the prior shift are documented and reviewed before the hand-off ends.

What if my parent needs more support than this?

We are not a nursing service. We do not administer medication, perform clinical procedures, or replace home-care nursing. For higher-acuity needs we coordinate with the family's nursing or care team, but the nursing itself stays with them.

Common questions

What if my parent has dementia or Alzheimer's?

We match Assistants with dementia experience at intake. Care is companion-level and routine-based; behavioural escalation requiring clinical intervention is outside our scope. We will tell you on the intake call whether we are the right fit for your parent's stage.

Do you administer medication?

No. The Assistant provides reminders at scheduled times, supervises self-administration if helpful, and documents missed doses. Medication management stays with the family and prescribing clinician.

Can my parent stay in their own home?

Yes. Our service is built around in-home care. We come to your parent.

Can we start short-term and extend if it's working?

Yes. Most senior contracts start with a defined window and extend with thirty days' notice. The same Assistant continues with your parent across the extension.

What about hospital-to-home transitions?

Bridge / Short-Term Live-In is designed for this. Coverage typically begins on discharge and runs two to six weeks. We coordinate with the discharge team if you'd like us to.

What if my parent and the Assistant don't click?

We rematch within the contract at no charge during the first month. After that, we discuss adjustments together — sometimes a fit problem is actually a routine problem.
