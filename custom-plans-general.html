Overview

General day-to-day support gives you a trusted person handling the moving parts of family life — errands, appointments, household coordination, family transportation, and project work. Think of it as a personal assistant for the family, in-home and ongoing.

Most families using this category have the standard things handled — childcare, eldercare, schedules — but want one steady person managing the friction. The Assistant fills the gap between what an executive assistant does in an office and what a household manager does at home.

What the Assistant helps with

  • Errands and shopping — groceries, pharmacy, gifts, returns
  • Appointment scheduling and transportation for the family
  • Household coordination — repairs, deliveries, vendor management
  • Family logistics — calendars, RSVPs, planning, reminders
  • Light household tasks — laundry, tidying, restocking
  • Occasional heavy housekeeping — deep cleans, furniture moves, one-off outdoor projects (typically once or twice a month, agreed in advance)
  • Pet care logistics — vet appointments, walks, daily routines
  • Project work — moves, renovations, events, hosting prep
  • Everything in between — the small things that never make the to-do list but always need doing

How the day looks

The Assistant arrives at an agreed time — typically 4 to 8 hours on weekdays. The day is structured around what the week actually demands rather than a fixed routine. You and the Assistant agree the priority list at the start of each week, and they work through it independently.

How tasks get assigned

Most families use a shared list — text, email, or a shared notes app. The Assistant works the list, flags blockers, and reports completion at the end of the day. We do not require any specific tool; we adapt to the family's existing communication.

Can the Assistant manage tradespeople and vendors?

Yes. Many families use us specifically for this — letting trades in, supervising work, signing for deliveries, coordinating between vendors. The Assistant is briefed on the project and acts as your point of contact during working hours.

What's the privacy expectation?

Confidentiality clauses are part of every contract. No personal information leaves the team. Discretion is part of why families hire us; we take it seriously.

Common questions

Is this the same as a personal assistant?

Similar role, in-home and family-oriented rather than office-based. The work overlaps with an EA's — calendar, errands, vendor coordination — but the day is built around family life rather than corporate workflow.

What if we travel — can the Assistant come?

Yes, with the travel add-on. Active hours at standard rate, downtime at reduced presence rate, travel costs (flights, hotel, meals, mileage) pass-through and pre-approved.

Are background checks done?

Every Assistant clears a criminal record check, references, and an in-person vetting interview before joining a family. Same standard on every contract.

Can the Assistant work independently in our home when we're not there?

Yes, once the relationship is established and you've agreed in writing. Many families use the Assistant during work hours specifically because they're at the office.

What if our needs are seasonal — busy in spring, quiet in summer?

Hours can flex up or down with thirty days' notice within the contract term. For very seasonal needs we sometimes structure shorter contracts back-to-back rather than one long one.

Can the Assistant handle confidential paperwork?

Yes — drop-offs, document signing, courier coordination. We do not provide legal, tax, or financial advice; for those we coordinate with your existing professionals.
