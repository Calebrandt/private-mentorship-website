Job Description

A Private Family Assistant works alongside the same family for the long term — supporting one client through education, life skills, and personal support across the rooms and programs that matter to them. You become the constant: in classrooms, at appointments, in the home, on outings.

This isn't a short-term gig. The clients we serve have been with Private Mentorship since 2023 and they're still here. We're hiring people who want to do this as a real career — not a stopgap.

You'll work as a self-employed contractor, set your own schedule with each family, and oscillate between in-person and online sessions across a flexible week.

How the Role Works

Three functions a Private Family Assistant carries that nobody else in the family's life provides at once.

Representation

You sit in the rooms — classrooms, WorkBC intakes, CLBC facilitator meetings, psychological assessments, workplace shifts — and speak for the client when they can't.

Resourcing

You find the programs, fill the funding applications, and build the support network the family didn't know existed — provincial, federal, community.

Reinforcement

You take notes in the room, write a clear report home to the family, then run a follow-up session with the client to re-teach what didn't land. The loop closes with you.

Key Duties & Responsibilities

Education

  • One-on-one teaching across school subjects, ESL, post-secondary prep, or hard skills the client is working on
  • Sit in on classes and programs (with consent), take structured notes, write a family-facing report
  • Re-teach material in a follow-up session so concepts stick
  • Coordinate with teachers, instructors, and program coordinators on the client's behalf
  • Help with applications: school, programs, scholarships, funding

Life Skills

  • Communication, socialization, conflict resolution — modeled in real situations, not lectures
  • Outings into the community: appointments, events, transit practice, leisure
  • Job search support: applications, interview prep, workplace check-ins with managers
  • Volunteer placement and ongoing support at the placement
  • Positive role-modeling across every interaction — this is the throughline of the role

Personal Support

  • Grocery shopping with or for the client; errands; transit support
  • Light home tasks (dishes, laundry, tidying, occasional cleaning) as part of daily living support
  • Family coordination — calendar, paperwork, communicating with siblings, parents, caseworkers
  • Medication reminders — never administering, no needles, no dosing decisions
  • Simple supportive tasks if requested by the family — never clinical procedures

Scope of this role. A Private Family Assistant is a supportive presence in a family's daily life — not a nurse or care aide. Some families ask for help with personal-care tasks beyond what's listed above. Those requests are handled privately, on a case-by-case basis, never as a default expectation of the role.

Compensation & Schedule

Pay: $21 – $45 / hour, depending on contract tier and tenure

Hours: Flexible. Sessions typically run 2–3 hours; you and the client co-create the schedule

Format: In-person and online sessions across Greater Vancouver

Term: Long-term, ongoing — most clients have been with us since 2023

What's included

  • Outings are not your expense — family pays for outings, transit, tickets, meals, and the assistant's portion when applicable
  • Raises and bonuses after a 3-month probation
  • Founding hires may earn full contract rate ($30–$45/hr) on selected contracts
  • Schedule flexibility — many assistants hold a 9–5 elsewhere and run evening sessions online

Qualifications

Education & Experience

  • Grade 12 / Canadian high school diploma (minimum)
  • 1+ year of relevant experience: education, mentorship, tutoring, caregiving, coaching, or similar
  • Hard skills are valuable however you got them — degrees, diplomas, or self-taught with proof
  • Tell us what you can teach: music, language, trades, sports, software, art, finance

Skills & Abilities

  • Strong written and verbal communication — you'll be writing reports families read
  • Reliable, organized, able to adapt when a session shifts mid-day
  • Comfortable around schools, caseworkers, and other professionals
  • Positive role-modeling as a default — clients pick up your tone before your words
  • Discretion — families share private things; that stays private

About Private Mentorship

Private Mentorship places one consented professional inside every room and program a vulnerable client must navigate, and brings all of it back to a family that can no longer run that coordination alone. We bridge gaps families face when the parents are aging, the siblings are stretched, and the existing system isn't built to assemble itself around one person.

We've supported clients through schools, WorkBC, CLBC, Back in Motion, psychological assessments, federal and provincial funding applications, and workplace placements. The same families have stayed with us since the business started.
